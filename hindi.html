Hindi

The Hindi Department came into being in 1965 as a one ­man Department, with Sri Harihar Tiwari and a few students. In 1966, Part Time Teaching started under the guidance of three teachers and the following year 1967 Sri Surya Kant Jha started teaching. Since 1965 sincere hardworking consequenced in good results in the following years and further progress was accomplished with increasing attendance of the students and devoted teaching and its methodology. In 1980 Mr. Tiwari retired after his 15 years of dedicated service. In 1993 Dr. S. K. Jha retired after running the Department solitarily, yet efficiently for 13 years. The same year Dr. Sunanda Raychowdhury gained appointment through College Service Commission and took up the responsibility of continuing the hardwork done by her predecessors on her own shoulders and much later the part­ timers appeared into the frame enhancing their sincere efforts to glory much brighter.

2001, the year when Hindi Honours was introduced. 2007, the first batch of Part ­ II achieved cent percent result and the glory kept on continuing the following years with persistence. In 2007 Prof. Mamata Trivedi joined the Department with her efforts to drag on the previous achievements to its heights and the following year 2008 Prof. Ajit Das, ushered in as an extra support to bolster the workforce.

Future Plans

To encourage and sustain active interest of the students the subject, apart from syllabus activity, by encouraging, promoting and organising-

1.       Guided tours to places of literacy interest.

2.       Theatre/poetry/creative writing workshops.

3.       To conceive and encourage short internships with authors, poets and journalist.

4.       To build a better reference library because literary material and reference books have to be normally obtained from outside the states.

5.       To encourage the college to subscribe to various literary magazines for the department.

6.       To organise visits and interactive sessions at places like NSD and SRFTI to encourage students to explore alternative career options.

7.       Students should be tech friendly: to be able to think on the key board.
