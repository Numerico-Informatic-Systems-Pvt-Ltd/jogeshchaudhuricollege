 NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME

  (REPORT OF SPECIAL CAMP PROGRAMME 2015)

  [28th JANUARY TO 3rd February 2015]

 

Special Camp for 2014-15 was organized at Jogesh Chandra Chaudhuri College from 28th January to 3rd February 2015. The annual event of the Special Camp organized by the Jogesh Chandra Chaudhuri College, NSS Units, was conducted with renewed experience and enthusiasm yet this year with many new components of social service added.

 Activities included teams of NSS student volunteers visiting chosen underprivileged sections of the locality, namely adopted slums, interacting with them, local NGO-s for identifying problems, collaborating with NGOs for addressing the problem, conducting awareness raising programs on identified themes which are problematic in that area and sharing materials on the same themes. Volunteers also surveyed the adopted slum door to door about various issues like education, child marriage, women right etc.  At the end of the camp, volunteers walked at rally with posters on Human rights Violation.

 This year the Camp was organized under the leadership of respected Teacher-in-Charge, Prof Durgadas Bhattacharyya, Jogesh Chandra Chaudhuri College and under the direct supervision of the four present Program Officers. This College has the heritage of superb unity within the four units when it comes to action. The four units have been holding yearlong General activities and Special Camps together – which is not only resource saving, but also needs exemplary team work.The NSS UNIT-I adopted a slum named BARABAGAN (WARD NO 94) at Anwar Shah Road consisting of about more than thousand settlements and having at least four clubs in the area.
